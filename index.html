My interests

Microscopy: super-resolution/volumetric/miniaturized/multi-photon imaging

I am focusing on developing advanced optical microscopy.
I am particularly interested in new imaging physics, bottom-up opto-electronic system design, as well as harnessing/designing optimal algorithms/optics for every specific application scenario.
Including:
Label-free: lensless/endoscopic/label-free-super-resolution/optical-diffraction-tomography microscopy;
Fluorescence: (miniaturized) light-field/multi-photon microscopy, and SIM/SOFI/SMLM.

Learn More

Machine Learning: low/high level computer vision and data science

Non-deep model: I am using the available prior knowledge to design specific algorithms to analyze and reconstruct the multi-dimensional bio/natural signals.
Including:
Deconvolution, denoise, phase retrieval, optical tracking, segmentation, and Bayesian inference.

Deep-learning: Taking advantage of priors from data, deep-learning has the fascinating character on solving the problems that can never be solved by conventional way (e.g., automatic feature extraction). Therefore, I intend to use deep-learning to reduce the photon/time/hardware budgets of microscopy. On the other hand, I am also fascinated on using deep-learning to speed up the data profiling pipeline of biologists.
Including:
Cross-modality, low-level transformation, segmentation, visual-relationship.

Sparse-SIM

Taking advantage of a priori knowledge of the sparsity and continuity of biological structures, we develop a deconvolution algorithm that extends twofold resolution beyond physical limits posed by any existing microscopic hardware for the first time. As a result, sparse structured illumination microscopy (Sparse-SIM) achieves ~60 nm resolution at a temporal resolvability of ~2 ms, allowing it to resolve intricate structures, including small vesicular fusion pores and relative movements between the inner and outer membranes of mitochondria in live cells.
