My interests

Microscopy

I am focusing on miniaturized, super-resolution and advanced 3D optical microscopy. I am particularly interested in new imaging physics, bottom-up opto-electronic system design, as well as new principles for light propagation, light-matter interaction and image formation in complex biological materials. Including (miniaturized) light-field microscopy, label-free super-resolution microscopy (S3IM), (miniaturized) faster, clearer and more robust (fluorescent) (super-resolution) microscopy (Confocal, light-field, SIM, SOFI and SMLM)

Deep-learning has the fascinating character on solving the problems that can never be solved by conventional way. Selected Projects

Bayes Light-field microscopy

Atifacts-free, diffraction-limit resolution, single-shot three-dimensional microscopy, the revolution on the live 3D biological imaging. (Mitochondria in 3D)

Sparse-SIM

Taking advantage of a priori knowledge of the sparsity and continuity of biological structures, we develop a deconvolution algorithm that extends twofold resolution beyond physical limits posed by any existing microscopic hardware for the first time. As a result, sparse structured illumination microscopy (Sparse-SIM) achieves ~60 nm resolution at a temporal resolvability of ~2 ms, allowing it to resolve intricate structures, including small vesicular fusion pores and relative movements between the inner and outer membranes of mitochondria in live cells.

Three-dimensional Spinning-disk confocal SIM (SD-SIM) imaging

Live-cell 3D image of the mitochondrial membrane in the division stage with a depth of field of up to ~7 µm. Mitochondrial expressions of the outer membrane marker TOM20-mCherry captured by SD-SIM and Sparse SD-SIM. The color-coded projection of the mitochondrial membrane in the z direction as the depth increases gradually and the y-z orthoslices along the x-axis.
