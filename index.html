My interests

Microscopy

I am focusing on miniaturized, super-resolution and advanced 3D optical microscopy. I am particularly interested in new imaging physics, bottom-up opto-electronic system design, as well as new principles for light propagation, light-matter interaction and image formation in complex biological materials, especially at the single-molecule level. Including (miniaturized) light-field microscopy, label-free super-resolution microscopy (S3IM), (miniaturized) faster, and more robust fluorescent super-resolution microscopy (Confocal, SIM, SOFI and STORM)

Learn More

Deep learning

Deep learning has the fascinating character on solving the problems that can never be solved by conventional way. Therefore I use deep learning to "extend the depth-of-field; reduce the reconstruction time (to ms scale); and enable fewer photons (100 fold)", in imaging, and analyze the biological information. Including GAN, CNN.

Learn More

Computional imaging

I am using the physics priors to analyze and reconstruct the images, and design new alghrithms to solve the image inverse problems better. Including bio-image and natural image. Including deconvolution, denoise, phase retrieval, optical tracking.

Learning based light-field microscopy

Comparison of L-LFM results against conventional LFM results (cGAN Input) and Bayes-LFM (Model-based reconstruction) results, as a function of axial position (Z). These results clearly demonstrate that the L-LFM can reconstruct over the axial range that it was trained for (i.e. -1≤ Z ≤ 2 μm in this case). Outside this training range (marked with red Z values), the network output is not reliable. Scale bar: 10 μm.
